# CCRN Master Assessment Rubric

## Evaluation Framework
**Purpose:** Standardize assessment of critical care nursing competency across clinical scenarios and knowledge domains.

## Scoring Matrix

### Level 4: Expert (CCRN-Ready)
**Characteristics:**
- Demonstrates immediate recognition of life-threatening patterns
- Prioritizes interventions using evidence-based algorithms without prompting
- Anticipates complications and implements preventive strategies
- Integrates multi-system assessments into cohesive clinical picture
- Mentors others while managing complex patient care

**Indicators:**
- Correctly identifies subtle ECG changes (Wellens, de Winter T-waves) and initiates appropriate protocols
- Adjusts ventilator settings based on P/F ratios and compliance curves without supervision
- Calculates and interprets complex hemodynamic values (CPO, CPI, SVV) to guide therapy
- Manages competing priorities in multi-system organ failure with clear rationale

### Level 3: Proficient (Approaching CCRN)
**Characteristics:**
- Recognizes common critical care presentations with minimal cues
- Follows protocols appropriately with occasional need for guidance
- Identifies obvious relationships between lab values and clinical status
- Requires minimal prompting for complex decision-making

**Indicators:**
- Interprets standard 12-lead ECGs and basic arrhythmias correctly
- Manages stable ventilator patients and recognizes weaning readiness
- Understands basic PA catheter values and their clinical implications
- Identifies sepsis criteria and initiates bundle elements appropriately

### Level 2: Advanced Beginner (Learning CCRN Content)
**Characteristics:**
- Requires prompting to recognize priority problems
- Follows checklists but struggles with prioritization when multiple issues present
- Identifies single-system problems but misses interactions
- Needs supervision for high-risk procedures and medication titrations

**Indicators:**
- Recognizes obvious STEMI but may miss NSTEMI equivalents
- Understands ventilator modes but struggles with alarm troubleshooting
- Can identify abnormal lab values but needs guidance on clinical significance
- Knows sepsis definition but delays antibiotic administration

### Level 1: Novice (Pre-CCRN Preparation)
**Characteristics:**
- Focuses on tasks rather than patient condition
- Requires constant supervision for critical care specific interventions
- Unable to anticipate needs or recognize subtle changes
- Knowledge is theoretical; application is inconsistent

**Indicators:**
- Difficulty distinguishing artifact from true arrhythmias
- Unable to troubleshoot ventilator alarms without assistance
- Confuses PA catheter values (e.g., mixes up PAOP with PAP)
- Delays recognition of deteriorating patient status

## Domain-Specific Criteria

### Cardiovascular Assessment
| Criteria | Expert (4) | Proficient (3) | Advanced Beginner (2) | Novice (1) |
|----------|------------|----------------|----------------------|------------|
| Hemodynamic Interpretation | Calculates derived values (SVR, CPO) and correlates with tissue perfusion | Identifies normal vs. abnormal values and basic trends | Recognizes numbers but struggles with clinical correlation | Cannot identify normal ranges |
| Arrhythmia Management | Anticipates treatment for unstable rhythms before decompensation | Implements ACLS protocols appropriately | Recognizes basic rhythms but delays treatment | Confuses lethal and non-lethal rhythms |
| Vasoactive Management | Independently titrates multiple agents based on dynamic assessment | Titrate single agents with protocol guidance | Requires prompting for dose adjustments | Cannot explain mechanism of action |

### Respiratory Assessment
| Criteria | Expert (4) | Proficient (3) | Advanced Beginner (2) | Novice (1) |
|----------|------------|----------------|----------------------|------------|
| Ventilator Management | Adjusts settings based on P/V loops and WOB assessment | Manages stable ventilated patients and SBTs | Recognues desaturation but struggles with cause | Cannot explain basic ventilator modes |
| ABG Interpretation | Anticipates compensation limits and mixed disorders | Identifies primary disorder and appropriate compensation | Identifies pH and CO2 abnormalities only | Cannot interpret ABG without assistance |

### Neurological Assessment
| Criteria | Expert (4) | Proficient (3) | Advanced Beginner (2) | Novice (1) |
|----------|------------|----------------|----------------------|------------|
| ICP Management | Balances sedation, CPP, and osmotherapy dynamically | Maintains ICP <20 and CPP >60 with protocol | Recognizes ICP elevation but delays treatment | Does not understand ICP waveform |
| Stroke Recognition | Identifies stroke mimics and contraindications to tPA | Recognizes stroke symptoms within window | Knows stroke signs but misses window considerations | Cannot identify stroke symptoms |

## Clinical Scenario Evaluation

### Scenario Complexity Levels
- **Level A:** Single system, stable patient (e.g., post-cardiac cath recovery)
- **Level B:** Single system, unstable (e.g., STEMI with cardiogenic shock)
- **Level C:** Multi-system, stable (e.g., ventilated patient with resolving sepsis)
- **Level D:** Multi-system, unstable (e.g., trauma with TBI, pulmonary contusion, and pelvic fracture)

### Passing Standards
- **CCRN Eligibility:** Consistent Level 3 performance across all domains with at least one Level 4 domain
- **Remediation Required:** Any Level 1 performance in cardiovascular, respiratory, or neurological domains
- **Clinical Clearance:** Level 3 or higher in emergency response scenarios (codes, rapid response)

## Documentation Requirements
All assessments must include:
1. Patient scenario complexity level
2. Specific observed behaviors mapped to rubric criteria
3. Rationale for scoring level assigned
4. Plan for advancement to next level
5. Reference to specific CCRN blueprint content domain
