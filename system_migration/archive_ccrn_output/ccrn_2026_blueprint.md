# CCRN 2026 Certification Blueprint

## Examination Overview
- **Total Items:** 150 (125 scored, 25 unscored pilot items)
- **Time Allotted:** 3 hours
- **Passing Standard:** Angoff method with subject matter expert consensus
- **Validity Period:** 3 years (recertification via CERPs or exam)

## Detailed Content Outline

### Cardiovascular (20% - 30 items)
#### Hemodynamic Monitoring
- Arterial line waveform interpretation
- Central venous pressure monitoring and clinical correlation
- Pulmonary artery catheter values (CVP, PAP, PAOP, CO/CI, SVR/PVR)
- Tissue perfusion assessment (lactate, SvO2/ScvO2, capillary refill)

#### Pathologies
- Acute coronary syndromes (STEMI/NSTEMI differentiation)
- Heart failure exacerbations (acute decompensated HF)
- Cardiogenic shock and mechanical circulatory support (IABP, Impella, ECMO)
- Hypertensive emergencies
- Aortic dissection and aneurysm management

#### Interventions
- Advanced arrhythmia interpretation and ACLS protocols
- Temporary and permanent pacemaker troubleshooting
- Cardioversion and defibrillation indications
- Vasoactive medication titration (norepinephrine, epinephrine, vasopressin, dobutamine)

### Respiratory (18% - 27 items)
#### Mechanical Ventilation
- Mode selection (AC/VC, AC/PC, SIMV, PS, APRV)
- PEEP titration and lung protective strategies
- Weaning parameters and spontaneous breathing trials
- Troubleshooting ventilator alarms and asynchrony

#### Pathologies
- ARDS diagnosis (Berlin criteria) and management (ARDSNet protocols)
- COPD exacerbation and asthma status asthmaticus
- Pulmonary embolism and infarction
- Pneumothorax and chest tube management
- Pulmonary hypertension and cor pulmonale

#### Procedures
- Bronchoscopy preparation and post-procedure monitoring
- Thoracentesis and chest tube insertion care
- Percutaneous tracheostomy management

### Neurological (14% - 21 items)
#### Intracranial Dynamics
- ICP monitoring (ventricular vs. parenchymal vs. subdural)
- Cerebral perfusion pressure calculation and maintenance (CPP = MAP - ICP)
- Herniation syndromes (uncal, central, tonsillar)
- CSF drainage protocols and infection prevention

#### Pathologies
- Ischemic stroke (tPA eligibility, window periods, BP management)
- Hemorrhagic stroke (SAH, IPH, IVH)
- Traumatic brain injury (GCS scoring, sedation holds)
- Status epilepticus management
- Meningitis/encephalitis (isolation precautions, antibiotic timing)

### Renal/Genitourinary (10% - 15 items)
- Acute kidney injury (RIFLE/AKIN criteria)
- Continuous renal replacement therapy (CRRT) modalities (CVVH, CVVHD, CVVHDF)
- Drug dosing adjustments in renal failure
- Electrolyte emergencies (hyperkalemia, hyponatremia, hypercalcemia)
- Acid-base disorders (anion gap metabolic acidosis, respiratory compensation)

### Gastrointestinal (8% - 12 items)
- Acute pancreatitis (Ranson criteria, BISAP score, necrosis management)
- Upper and lower GI bleeding (Rockall score, Blatchford score)
- Hepatic failure and encephalopathy (ammonia management, INR monitoring)
- Mesenteric ischemia recognition
- Abdominal compartment syndrome monitoring

### Endocrine (8% - 12 items)
- Diabetic ketoacidosis (anion gap closure, fluid resuscitation protocols)
- Hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state (HHS)
- Thyroid storm and myxedema coma
- Adrenal crisis (Addisonian crisis) and Cushing's syndrome
- SIADH vs. DI vs. cerebral salt wasting

### Hematology/Immunology (7% - 10 items)
- Disseminated intravascular coagulation (ISTH criteria)
- Heparin-induced thrombocytopenia (4T score, serotonin release assay)
- Transfusion reactions (acute hemolytic, TRALI, TACO, allergic)
- Sepsis (Sepsis-3 definitions, qSOFA, lactate clearance)
- Oncologic emergencies (tumor lysis syndrome, SVC syndrome)

### Multisystem (10% - 15 items)
- Sepsis and septic shock (hour-1 bundle compliance)
- Multiple organ dysfunction syndrome (SOFA score trending)
- Trauma (primary and secondary surveys, damage control resuscitation)
- Burns (Parkland formula, inhalation injury, compartment syndromes)
- Hypothermia and hyperthermia (targeted temperature management post-cardiac arrest)

### Behavioral/Psychosocial (5% - 8 items)
- Delirium assessment (CAM-ICU, RASS scoring)
- Alcohol and benzodiazepine withdrawal (CIWA-Ar, DT prevention)
- PTSD recognition in ICU survivors
- Family-centered care and shared decision-making
- Palliative care integration and withdrawal of life support

## Cognitive Levels
- **Remember/Understand:** 30% - Basic recall of facts and principles
- **Apply:** 50% - Clinical application of knowledge to scenarios
- **Analyze/Synthesize:** 20% - Complex decision-making and prioritization

## Item Types
- Multiple choice (4 options, single best answer)
- Multiple response (select all that apply)
- Hotspot (identify anatomical locations or ECG segments)
- Ordered response (drag-and-drop prioritization)
