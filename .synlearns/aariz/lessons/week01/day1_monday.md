## Lesson Block 1: Letters (20 min) — 9:10

### Today's Letter: O — "O is for Optimus"

**Introduction (5 min):**
> "Today we're learning the letter O. Can you guess which Autobot starts with O? OPTIMUS PRIME! The greatest leader of the Autobots starts with THIS letter."

- Draw a big O in the air with your finger. Have Aariz copy.
- "O is round, like Optimus Prime's wheels when he's in truck mode!"

**Letter Hunt (5 min):**
- Walk around the room together. Find things that start with O.
- "O... orange! O... outside! O... open the door!"
- If he struggles, give the sound: "Ah... like 'Ahhh-ptimus.' What else sounds like 'ah'?"

**Letter Writing Practice (10 min):**
> Use Activity Sheet 1 (attached). Trace the letter O, then try writing it independently.
> "Start at the top, go around like a wheel. Optimus Prime's wheel! Round and round."

**Process praise examples:**
- "You kept your pencil on the line — that takes control!"
- "I see you trying it again after it didn't look right — that's what engineers do."
- NOT: "Good job!" or "You're so smart!"

## Hands-On Activity: Building Optimus (20 min) — 9:30

### The Challenge:
> "Optimus Prime needs a new vehicle mode. Can you build him a truck out of Legos? It needs to have at LEAST 4 wheels and be big enough for this figure to ride on top."

**Scaffolding (if needed):**
- "What shape is a truck? Long like a rectangle!"
- "Where do the wheels go? On the bottom — two in front, two in back"
- "How will Optimus stay on top? Maybe a flat platform?"

**Extension (if too easy):**
- "Can you make it so Optimus can TRANSFORM? Robot mode AND truck mode?"
- "Can you add a trailer?"

**When he finishes:**
- "Tell me about your build. What does this part do? Why did you put this here?"
- Take a photo for the portfolio. Date it.
- "You PLANNED it, you BUILT it, and you can tell me HOW it works. That's engineering."

**DO NOT:**
- Build it for him
- Tell him it's wrong
- Rush him

## Lesson Block 2: STEM — Ramps and Speed (20 min) — 10:00

### Concept: Inclined Planes (Ramps)

**Setup:** Get a flat board, book, or piece of cardboard. Prop one end on a stack of books. Get a toy car.

**Experiment (15 min):**
1. "Autobots need ramps to launch! Let's build one."
2. Stack 2 books. Roll the car down. "How far did it go?"
3. Mark where it stopped with a crayon or block.
4. Stack 4 books. "What do you THINK will happen if the ramp is taller?"
5. Let him predict BEFORE rolling. Write or draw his prediction.
6. Roll it. Mark where it stopped.
7. "Was your prediction right? The taller ramp made the car go FURTHER! Why?"
8. "Because gravity pulls harder on a steep ramp. Gravity is the invisible force that pulls everything DOWN."

**Key vocabulary:** ramp, steep, gentle, farther, prediction, gravity

**Connection:**
> "When Optimus transforms and drives off a cliff in the movie, gravity pulls him down the ramp. Now you know WHY he goes so fast! The steeper the ramp, the faster the Autobot."

## Parent Notes for Shazeema

**What Aariz is actually learning today:**
- Letter O: visual recognition, sound correspondence (/o/ as in "on"), motor control for writing
- Engineering design: planning, building, explaining — the EiE cycle
- Physics: inclined planes, gravity, prediction vs. observation (scientific method foundations)
- Spatial reasoning: 3D Lego → 2D drawing translation

**Watch for:**
- Does he hold the pencil with a proper tripod grip? If not, don't force it — just model it
- When building, does he plan first or just start? Either is fine at this age — planning will develop
- During the ramp experiment, does he predict before testing? Prediction is the foundation of scientific thinking

**If he gets frustrated:**
- "It's OK to feel frustrated. That means your brain is growing."
- Take a break. Come back to it. Never force completion.
- Switch to something he loves (free building) and return to the challenge tomorrow.

**Read-aloud tonight (20 min):** Any book he chooses. Stop and ask "what do you think happens next?" once or twice. That's prediction practice — same as the ramp experiment but with stories.
