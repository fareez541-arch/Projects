## Lesson Block 1: Shapes (20 min) — 9:10

### Today's Shapes: Circle, Square, Triangle, Rectangle + NEW: Hexagon

**Shape Review (5 min):**
- Draw each shape. "What's this? A circle! Like Optimus's wheels."
- "Square — like the Allspark cube... well, one FACE of the cube."
- "Triangle — like the warning signs the Autobots use."
- "Rectangle — like a door, or Optimus in truck mode from the side."

**New Shape — Hexagon (5 min):**
> "Today we're learning a new shape the Autobots use. It has SIX sides. It's called a HEXAGON."
- Draw it. Count the sides together. "1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6! Six sides."
- "The Autobot shield is shaped like a hexagon! It's the strongest shape for armor."
- "Can you find anything shaped like a hexagon?" (Hex bolts, honeycomb patterns, some floor tiles)

**Shape Hunt (5 min):**
- Walk around the room. "Find me a circle... a rectangle... a triangle."
- "Bonus: can you find something with MORE than 4 sides?" (This is hard — celebrate any attempt)

**Shape Attributes (5 min):**
> "How are a square and a rectangle DIFFERENT?" (Square has all sides the same. Rectangle has 2 long and 2 short.)
> "How are they the SAME?" (Both have 4 sides and 4 corners.)
- This comparison skill is the foundation of classification and analytical thinking.

## Hands-On Activity: Pattern Code-Breaking (20 min) — 9:30

### "The Autobot Secret Code"

**Setup:** Cut out or use Lego bricks in 3 colors (red, blue, yellow).

**Pattern Level 1 — Copy (5 min):**
- Lay out: RED, BLUE, RED, BLUE, RED, BLUE
- "This is an Autobot code! Can you copy this pattern?"
- He builds the same pattern below yours.

**Pattern Level 2 — Extend (5 min):**
- Lay out: RED, RED, BLUE, RED, RED, BLUE, RED, RED, ___
- "What comes next in the code? What would an Autobot put here?"
- "How did you figure that out?" (Make him explain his reasoning!)

**Pattern Level 3 — Create (5 min):**
- "Now YOU are the code maker. Create a secret code pattern for ME to crack."
- He makes a pattern. You "try" to extend it. Let him tell you if you're right.
- If you're "wrong," let him correct you. Teaching is learning.

**Pattern Level 4 — Translate (5 min, stretch goal):**
- Take the color pattern and turn it into sounds: RED=clap, BLUE=stomp
- "CLAP, STOMP, CLAP, STOMP" — "It's the same pattern, but with SOUNDS instead of COLORS!"
- This is abstraction — recognizing that the same pattern exists in different forms. This is how engineers think.

## Lesson Block 2: Shape Engineering (20 min) — 10:00

### "Build the Strongest Bridge"

**Concept:** Different shapes have different strength. Triangles are the strongest shape in engineering.

**Experiment:**
1. Fold a piece of paper into a tube (cylinder). Stand it up. Put a small book on top. "How much can it hold?"
2. Fold a piece of paper into a triangle shape (triangular prism). Stand it up. Put the same book on top.
3. Fold paper into a square tube. Test it.
4. "Which shape held the most weight? Which shape is STRONGEST?"

**The reveal:**
> "Engineers use TRIANGLES because they don't collapse! Look at bridges — they're full of triangles. That's not an accident. Engineers chose the strongest shape."

**Building challenge:**
> "Can you build a bridge out of Lego that uses TRIANGLE shapes? It needs to hold this toy car."

**If this is too abstract:** That's OK. Just build any bridge and test if the car can cross. The triangle concept will return in later weeks.

## Creative Station (20 min) — 10:20

### Shape Collage Art — "Autobot Portrait"

**Activity:**
1. Pre-cut (or have him cut) shapes from colored paper: circles, squares, triangles, rectangles
2. "Can you make an AUTOBOT using only shapes? Glue them on paper."
3. Rectangle body, circle wheels, triangle arms/weapons, hexagon head

**Activity Sheet 1 (attached):** Shape identification worksheet — circle the hexagons, cross out the shapes that are NOT triangles.

**Activity Sheet 2 (attached):** Pattern completion — fill in the missing piece of each Autobot code pattern.

**The collage is the important one.** It teaches: shape recognition, spatial arrangement, composition, fine motor (cutting, gluing), and creative problem-solving (how do I make a robot from just shapes?).

## Parent Notes for Shazeema

**What Aariz is actually learning today:**
- Geometry: shape names, attributes (sides, corners), shape comparison (same/different)
- Pattern recognition: the foundation of algebraic thinking and coding
- Abstraction: same pattern in different forms (color → sound) = computational thinking
- Structural engineering: triangles as the strongest shape (real engineering principle)
- Fine motor + spatial reasoning: cutting shapes, composing a collage

**Watch for:**
- Can he identify shapes reliably? Squares and rectangles are commonly confused — that's normal
- During pattern work, does he "see" the repeating unit? If not, help him group them: "RED-BLUE is one unit. RED-BLUE is another. What's the next unit?"
- During the bridge test, does he predict? Even if wrong, predicting = scientific thinking

**Key phrases to use today:**
- "What do you NOTICE about these shapes?" (not "this is a...")
- "How did you FIGURE that out?" (make him explain his thinking)
- "What would happen IF...?" (encourage prediction)

**Read-aloud tonight:** Any book. Point out shapes in the illustrations. "I see a triangle in that roof! Can you find a circle on this page?"
