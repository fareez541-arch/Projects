## Lesson Block 1: Phonics (20 min) — 9:10

### Today's Focus: Letter Sounds — B, M, S + Review O

**Letter B — "B is for Bumblebee" (5 min):**
- Write a big B. "This letter says /b/. Like the start of Bumblebee."
- "B... B... Bumblebee. B... B... Ball. B... B... Building!"
- "Can you think of another word that starts with /b/?" Let him try. Accept any attempt.
- Practice writing B on Activity Sheet 1.

**Letter M — "M is for Megatron" (5 min):**
- Write a big M. "This letter says /m/. Like Megatron."
- "M... M... Megatron. M... M... Mama! M... M... Moon!"
- Practice writing M.
- "M has mountains in it! Two bumps on top."

**Letter S — "S is for Starscream" (5 min):**
- Write a big S. "This letter says /sss/. Like a snake sound! Like Starscream."
- "S... S... Starscream. S... S... Sun. S... S... Six!"
- Practice writing S.
- "S is curvy, like a snake slithering."

**Sound Matching Game (5 min):**
- "I'll say a word. You tell me — does it start with B, M, or S?"
- "BOOK" (B!) "STAR" (S!) "MILK" (M!) "BALL" (B!) "SNAKE" (S!) "MOUSE" (M!)
- Keep it fast and fun. High-five for each correct answer.
- If he gets one wrong, don't correct immediately. Say the word slowly, emphasizing the first sound: "Mmmmm-ouse. Mmmmm... what letter says mmmm?"

## Hands-On Activity: Sound Scavenger Hunt (15 min) — 9:30

### "Autobot Intel: Sound Recon"

**Mission:**
> "Bumblebee lost his voice (just like in the movies!). He needs to find things that start with his letter sound to get it back. Find 3 things in this room that start with /b/."

- He hunts. When he finds one, he brings it to the "base."
- Do the same for /m/ and /s/.
- Lay out all the objects. Sort them into 3 groups by starting sound.
- "You just decoded the Autobot sound system!"

**This teaches:** phonemic awareness (isolating initial sounds), categorization, active movement, and connecting abstract sounds to real objects.

## Lesson Block 2: Engineering Design Challenge (25 min) — 9:55

### "Build a Launcher for Bumblebee"

**The Problem:**
> "Bumblebee needs to launch himself over this wall (a book standing up). Can you build a launcher that sends this ball over the wall?"

**Materials:** Cardboard tubes, rubber bands, tape, a small ball or crumpled paper ball, a box, a stick/pencil for the lever arm.

**Engineering Design Process (guide him through each step):**

**1. ASK (2 min):**
> "What is the problem? Bumblebee needs to get OVER the wall. What do we need? Something that LAUNCHES."

**2. IMAGINE (3 min):**
> "What could we build? A catapult? A ramp? A slingshot?" 
> Let him brainstorm. Draw quick sketches of his ideas. Don't judge any idea.

**3. PLAN (3 min):**
> "Which idea do you want to try first? What materials do you need?"
> Help him gather materials. Let him choose the approach.

**4. CREATE (12 min):**
> He builds. You assist ONLY when asked or when safety requires it.
> If he gets stuck: "What if you tried..." instead of "Do this."
> If it's not working: "What part isn't doing what you want? What could we change?"

**5. IMPROVE (5 min):**
> Test it. Did the ball go over?
> "What worked? What didn't? Can you make it go HIGHER? FARTHER?"
> This iteration step is where the deepest learning happens. Failure here is GOOD.

**Process praise:**
- "You tested it and it didn't work, so you changed the angle. That's what real engineers do."
- "I love that you tried 3 different designs before it worked."

**Parent note:** The PRODUCT doesn't matter. A crumpled paper launcher that barely works is a success if he went through the design process. The THINKING is the goal.

## Parent Notes for Shazeema

**What Aariz is actually learning today:**
- Phonics: letter-sound correspondence for B, M, S (the foundation of reading)
- Phonemic awareness: isolating initial sounds — the #1 predictor of reading success
- Engineering design process: the 5-step cycle used by actual engineers
- Iteration: testing, failing, improving — growth mindset in practice
- Fine motor: letter writing, building with small materials

**Watch for:**
- Can he hear the difference between beginning sounds? This is harder than it seems for 4-year-olds
- If he mixes up B and D (common): "B has its belly in the FRONT" (trace a lowercase b — the bump is on the right)
- During building, does he give up after one failed attempt? Coach through: "What PART didn't work? Just that part — the rest was good."

**Important today:**
The engineering challenge might be hard. He might get frustrated. That's the POINT. How he handles frustration — with your support — is more important than whether the launcher works. Model calm problem-solving: "Hmm, that didn't work. Let me think... what if we try THIS?"

**Read-aloud tonight:** While reading, point to words that start with B, M, S, O. "There's a B word! What sound does it start with?" Don't overdo it — 3-4 times per book is plenty.
