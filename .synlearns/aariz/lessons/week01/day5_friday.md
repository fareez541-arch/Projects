## Review Game: "Cybertron Quiz Show" (20 min) — 9:10

### Make it a GAME, not a test.

**Setup:** He sits in a special chair (the "Command Chair"). You're the quiz host. Use a silly voice if you want. Every answer (right or wrong) gets a point. There are no wrong answers on Free Build Friday — only "almost" answers.

**Round 1 — Letters (5 min):**
- "What letter is this?" (Draw O, B, M, S on paper cards)
- "What SOUND does this letter make?"
- "Tell me a word that starts with /b/... /m/... /s/..."
- "Can you write the letter O? B?"

**Round 2 — Numbers (5 min):**
- "Count as high as you can!" (See where he gets to naturally)
- "What comes after 12? After 14?"
- Show fingers: "How many fingers am I holding up?" (do 10+)
- "If you have 5 Energon cubes and I give you 1 more, how many?"

**Round 3 — Shapes (5 min):**
- Draw shapes. "What's this called?"
- "How many sides does a hexagon have?"
- "What's the strongest shape for building?" (Triangle!)
- "How are a square and rectangle DIFFERENT?"

**Round 4 — Science & Engineering (5 min):**
- "What happened when we made the ramp taller?" (Car went farther)
- "What invisible force pulls things down?" (Gravity)
- "When your launcher didn't work, what did you do?" (Changed it / tried again)
- "What are the steps? Ask, Imagine, Plan, Create, Im...?" (Improve!)

**Scoring:** 
> "You scored [big number] points! That's more than Megatron scored all YEAR. You are officially promoted from Cadet to... SCOUT RANK!"

Create a simple "certificate" if you have time — a piece of paper that says "CYBERTRON ACADEMY — Scout Rank — Awarded to Aariz for completing Week 1." He'll keep it forever.

## FREE BUILD TIME (40 min) — 9:30

### This is the most important part of the week.

**The rule:** Aariz chooses what to build. No assignment. No objective. No instructions.

**Your role:**
- "Today you build whatever you want. You're the engineer. What do you want to create?"
- Sit nearby. Be available. Don't direct.
- If he asks for help: help. If he doesn't: observe quietly.
- Ask open-ended questions ONLY if he invites conversation:
  - "Tell me about what you're building."
  - "What does this part do?"
  - "What gave you that idea?"

**Why this matters:**
Free building is where creativity lives. All week, the lessons were guided. Today, he integrates everything without structure. You'll see him use things he learned — patterns, shapes, counting — without being told to. That's transfer. That's real learning.

**Document it:**
- Take photos of what he builds. From multiple angles.
- If he'll talk about it, record a short video of him explaining.
- These go in the portfolio. This IS the evidence of learning.

**If he builds something related to this week's lessons** (uses triangles for strength, builds something with ramps, sorts by color) — note it but don't make a big deal. Internal motivation > external validation.

**If he builds something completely unrelated** — that's equally good. Creativity doesn't follow lesson plans.

## Creative Station: Art + Reflection (20 min) — 10:10

### "Draw Your Week"

**Activity:**
- Give him a big piece of paper (or tape 2 together).
- "Draw your favorite thing from this week. It can be your launcher, your ramp experiment, your Autobot pattern code, your Lego truck, your nature observation — anything!"
- Let him draw freely. No corrections. No prompts about what to include.
- When done: "Tell me about your drawing." Write down what he says (his words, exactly) on the back of the paper. Date it.

**Activity Sheet 1 (attached):** Autobot coloring page — detailed enough to be engaging, simple enough to complete. Just for fun. No learning objective. It's Friday.

**Activity Sheet 2 (attached):** "My Week at Cybertron Academy" — simple fill-in: "This week I learned about the letter ___, the number ___, and the shape ___. My favorite day was ___." He fills in with drawing or writing (whatever he can do). Shazeema can write his spoken words if he can't write them yet.
